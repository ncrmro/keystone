## Your Documents, Photos, and Voice Messages Now Organize Themselves — Without Leaving Your Network

People who run their own infrastructure can now search photos by who's in them, extract tax data from PDFs, and turn voice messages into searchable notes — all processed locally by Keystone OS agents.

**Current state.** Self-hosters accumulate unstructured data — tax PDFs, voice messages, photos, screenshots — scattered across disconnected services. Finding anything requires manually remembering where it is, and there is no way to search by face or transcribe a memo without sending data to a cloud service.

**Why this.** The infrastructure already exists — photo storage, contacts, notes sync — but nothing connects them. An OS agent that can automate your tax prep is useless if it cannot read a PDF or transcribe a voice message.

**How.** Keystone gives agents the ability to read documents, hear voice messages, and search photos. PDFs are converted to searchable text with exact page-and-line citations so every extracted number traces back to its source. Photos and screenshots are indexed by face and text, and recognized people link directly into your address book.

**What.** An agent reads your brokerage PDFs and calculates capital gains with cited sources, transcribes voice messages and files key points into your notes, and finds every photo containing a specific person. It reconstructs what you were working on from screenshots, calls, and documents — then syncs a summary into your notes automatically. Everything runs on your hardware.

### FAQ
- **Q: Does any data leave my network?**
  A: No. Document parsing, transcription, and photo indexing all run locally.

- **Q: Do I need a GPU?**
  A: GPU acceleration improves speed but is not required. All processing works on CPU.

- **Q: What document formats are supported?**
  A: Scanned documents use text recognition, native PDFs are parsed directly. Page-and-line citations work with both.
